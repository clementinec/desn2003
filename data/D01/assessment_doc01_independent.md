## Assessment Basis

This assessment was built from the course-side Doc 0.1 expectations in `assessment.qmd` and `data/feedback_principles.txt`, not from the existing D01 assessment writeup. The quality half evaluates:

- thematic synthesis rather than source-by-source summary
- relevance and credibility of sources
- critical evaluation of limitations, methods, or blind spots
- whether the review clearly builds toward a research gap

The effort half evaluates:

- breadth of reading and evidence of actual literature searching
- completeness and development of the review
- structural care, citation preparation, and overall polish

**Curve method:** piecewise-linear mapping from the raw cohort distribution to the requested anchors.  
Raw min `13 -> 75`, raw median `67 -> 85`, raw max `89 -> 96`.

---

## Cohort Summary

This cohort is split into three fairly clear bands. The top band already understands that a literature review is an argument about a field rather than a list of sources. Those submissions organize the reading into themes, compare traditions or methods, and arrive at a gap that feels earned. The middle band usually has a workable topic and some relevant reading, but the writing still slips back into summary, the critical lens is inconsistent, or the evidence base is thinner than it needs to be. The lower band is mainly a completeness problem: too few sources, too little writing, or a document that is closer to a proposal than to a review.

**Common strengths**
- Most students have a real topic rather than a vague area of interest.
- Several students are already trying to identify methodological or conceptual blind spots, not just reporting findings.
- Many reviews have the beginnings of a usable Doc 1 direction even when the current draft is underdeveloped.

**Common weaknesses**
- Summary still dominates synthesis in much of the cohort.
- Source bases are often too small for a literature review; several submissions sit closer to `2-6` sources than the expected `8-12`.
- Critical evaluation is the most common missing move: many papers report findings but do not ask what the cited work could not answer.
- Gap sections are often asserted rather than demonstrated.

## Individual Evaluations

### 1. Li Ping Sum - Curved 96

**Topic:** FOMO and nostalgia-based emotional branding strategies driving emotional consumption across Generation Z  
**Quality: 43/50 | Effort: 46/50 | Raw: 89**

**What worked**
- This is one of the few submissions that really behaves like a literature review: it moves through concepts, mechanisms, and market strategies in a deliberate sequence.
- The reading effort is substantial. There is clear evidence of broad searching across academic, industry, and applied sources.
- The gap is more than a generic "more research is needed" claim; it identifies a reciprocal loop between Gen Z traits and branding strategy.

**What needs work**
- Some of the definitional and trend sources are too lightweight for core conceptual claims, and at least a few references look mismatched or shaky.
- The brand-strategy section becomes more descriptive than critical. It explains what brands are doing better than it evaluates the evidence behind those claims.

**Best next move for Doc 1**
- Audit the references carefully, replace the weakest definitional sources, and add a more explicit evaluative lens to the brand-strategy section.
