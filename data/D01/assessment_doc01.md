### 1. Li Ping Sum — Curved: 96

**Topic:** FOMO and nostalgia-based emotional branding strategies driving emotional consumption across Generation Z

**Quality: 44/50 | Effort: 46/50 | Raw: 90**

**What you did well:**
- This is a properly built literature review. The structure — moving from defining emotional consumption, to Gen Z characteristics, to FOMO and nostalgia as distinct drivers, to brand strategy across four industry categories, to a clear research gap — shows you understand what a lit review is supposed to do.
- Excellent source range: approximately 28–30 references spanning academic journals (Richins, Przybylski, Wildschut, Elhai), industry reports (BCG, McKinsey/BOF), and trade sources. Far exceeds the 8–12 target.
- The thematic synthesis is genuinely strong. The FOMO section, for example, integrates Przybylski's psychological framework with Elhai's reinforcement cycle and Argan's consumption model to build a layered argument. That's exactly how synthesis should work.

**Where to improve:**
- Some definitional sources are lightweight (e.g., a marketing blog for "emotional consumption," MBA Skool dictionary for "hedonic consumption"). For core definitions, peer-reviewed sources carry more weight.
- The brand strategy section (2.4) reads more like a market landscape scan than a critical review — it describes what brands do without evaluating evidence of whether these strategies actually work.
- At least two citations appear mismatched: Nations (2022) links to a marine litter report, and Doob (1950) is about Goebbels' propaganda, not Uses & Gratifications theory. Audit your reference list carefully.

**For Doc 1:** Fix the citation errors, replace lightweight definitional sources with peer-reviewed equivalents, and add critical evaluation to the brand strategy section.
